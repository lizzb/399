My goal in this study is to develop a mobile app that can both ease and enhance the experience of the Career Fair, specifically the SWE iDay career fair that happens at Northwestern every October. I was interested in this because I have personally attended this twice and it always pretty chaotic. I had hacked up a small scheduling tool for myself for my own purposes and just that experience gave myself more insight into what resources were lacking. Additionally, I have been to career fairs at the Grace Hopper conference for the last two years and those conferences were supported by conference apps. Additionally, I have been to the Google I/O conference, which was also supported by a conference app. So mobile event/conference apps have been really increasing in the past couple of years and is definitely an area of interest for me. I got a chance to explore this in a small bit this summer at a hack-a-thon at Google, but that has just whetted my appetite to explore this in more depth.

Click the button to reveal the full, verbose version of my current status. I have attempted to summarize it in bullet points below (specifically the feature spec section), but have not yet made a more concise version of all points:

I think the ability to sort, search and filter companies by location and by size of company is an important feature of a job fair. Currently, what is provided is the name of the company, what majors they are seeking, full time versus internship and depth of education (undergraduate degree versus doctorate). Also what would be desired to be included is a categorization of the industry of the company, the size of the company, locations they are hiring for as well as miscellaneous information (such as a focus on diversity).

I was curious whether Northwestern rewards long time participants at the career fair for their support. So the possibility to have QR codes for the employers to scan students would be useful. When you log in, you would register with your student ID and you would have a QR code generated. Perhaps the recruiters could use this information to see how many students stopped by their booths. It would also be nice if it could convey student information to the recruiter, although this might have invasion of privacy issues that would need to be resolved. Perhaps a student signing a waiver would be sufficient.

Pre-event engagement could definitely be improved. If you had a profile, such as Career Cat or McCormick Connect as well as Linked In (not provided by Northwestern), this could be accessed. For example, at the Tech Expo, which was yesterday, we got emails encouraging us to attend, but with pre-registration this notification could be more targeted. It would be great if this feature could be within the app, perhaps reminding you to update your resume on line. Although privacy issues would have to be sorted, the students could choose which information they would want to be made public, and the recruiters could sort by this information to make their jobs easier as well. This might help the recruiters connect a bit more. Many of these conference apps have built in contact lists so that you can create messaging features to meet up with people for dinner for example, but that might be overkill for career day as it is not long enough.

At Grace Hopper you upload your resume in advance and sometimes booths will contact you in advance with a reminder to ask that you visit. Pictures also can be uploaded in advance. Uploading recruiter pictures is especially helpful. For me, I have difficulty remembering names and faces, so having pictures of the recruiters allowed me to remember my interaction with them. Also, the ability to have a platform to take notes would be helpful, especially if a recruiter told you something specific, as this minimizes the distraction of pens and papers and allows you to focus on the brief time available for the recruiter interaction.

It is reasonable now to assume that all student have mobile devices and use them. On the company side, will they have the same computer horsepower as students? I will have to assume yes here. This app could also facilitate live tweets or periodic notifications of how much time is left until the fair is over. Many times at career fairs there is not ready access to clocks and the long lines lend themselves to keeping you off pace to accomplish what you wanted.

An app can help you make a mental check list of what you need before and after the career fair. It can prompt you for whether an hour in advance whether you have your resume, are you wearing the proper attire (such as closed toed shoes), which would be a nice touch. What would be desirable, although I am not sure now how it could be incorporated into the app, is if there were a way to identify what kind of student you were to the recruiter. This would be like the electronic version of a colored ribbon or name tag, so the recruiter could see students of the desired major in the crowd, since each company knows what majors it is looking for (but this is outside the scope of my app).

Slogging through our u.northwestern.edu email is tedious for devising a schedule, so an alternate way of doing this and organizing your day and creating a map for yourself would be desirable. If I were getting paid for this app, it would focus on both sets of users, both recruiters and students. However, as an unpaid project with limited scope, I would choose to focus on the students, who with their more tech savvy bent in general, are the more likely users anyway. Also, there are more students visiting than there are recruiters or planners, so the numbers would dictate the first pass of developing this application aim for the larger population.

What I believe is out of scope is the planning experience for Ellen or SWE members and how to import data easily for non-tech people. But I think I may have a method for importing data from a previous project, so this might be able to be added.

The app should also minimize internet usage. The app should not always have to connect to the internet. In large settings like Grace Hopper, massive internet use bogs down the system locally. For this app, that may not be relevant as it is a much smaller event. The Northwestern network in Norris can likely support the number of attendees at these types of events.

What is also out of scope is metrics of attendance or ratings of specific booths. Even though this is possible, and may have some prototyping, I did not intend to produce the functionality to support this. This would be the same for integration with Career Cat.

How else could this app improve the experience of the Career Fair in ways are beyond a basic functionality. One way would be line length checker. If you were about to head to Facebook, it might be nice if it were full, to be able to go to a shorter line with another company, but the mechanics for that might not be possible.

The app should have the ability to star your favorite companies. The app should also have a default searching profile so that every time you go to a list of companies you don’t continue to plug in the same search function. Because the information is available on company names, the list will be filterable, searchable and sortable. Each listing will open up into a page featuring the company. It will have their logo for identification, a link to the company website, either a recruiting link or a generic company link would be possible. The ability to filter by job type and major will be part of the app, but not the way that is currently done, which is an awkward excel spreadsheet based format with every major listed followed by X’s, which is visually difficult to use. Sorting alphabetically ascending and descending and being able to actively sort by table location ascending and descending would allow the student to optimize their time spent at the event. Sorting by room would also be part of the app. Anything that is in the paper handouts given to students would at least be covered in the mobile app, as the goal is to make the paper handouts eventually obsolete. The app will have to work on a number of devices, so I will have to check emulators to make sure this is covered. Using QR codes will require use of camera functionality, but that is pretty much standard in mobile devices.

Week 2-3 (Jan 13-17, Jan 20-24)

Week 2-3 - I made wireframes using Balsamic, looked into current popular tools for wireframing and creating interactive prototypes; realized that I was not the target user of these tools; will be able to skip the prototyping phase because I have skills to make a functional website (whereas these tools are made for designers such as art students without this experience); had some reaction (negative) to use of Balsamic (seemed to be faster in power point); need to investigate whether industry standard (use of a specific tool) is more important than speed of development; rediscovered tools from the Google I/O conference that are specific for UX design for developers; these were helpful but made me realize that doing design in a vacuum is difficult, clashing with this being an independent study. Therefore, will be cutting some design corners. Also researched HTML5 and UI framework and native/mobile/hybrid frameworks which are used in combination to allow in develop in HTML, javascript and CSS. These allow one to develop for the web but allow you to make external APIs that can be put into app stores and downloadable to a mobile phone platform without needing to use the internet, eliminating the need for a robust internet connection. This allows you to deploy to Android IOS, Windowsphone platform, etc. while not having to develop new code each time. I realized that there are a lot more options out there than I thought there would be and spent some time trying to find which would be best for me, but realized I need some guidance. For now, I will stick to using web dev tools as that will apply to almost any framework and is faster for prototyping. Additionally, included in exploring these frameworks was exploring different UI frameworks and while I am vaguely familiar with responsive web page design principles, I will likely utilize Bootstrap 3 to minimize some of the testing efforts and making sure the page displays correctly on a number of devices. I did debate using Foundation but Bootstrap 3 will provide room for growth as I am familiar with Bootstrap 2, but there are newer features and also extensive on line community for questions and in terms of a framework, whatever I use will utilize JQuery mobile and although I have some reservations in speed of programming with this tool, it is also robust and has a wide community of users. Stress testing the application is not a high priority for this assignment as response time is not critical. I also determined some things that were out of scope. Due to time constraints for the project, I will focus on the experience of students at the career and not including the organizers of the event. I also recognized that some features I may prototype but not actually make functional, such as logins.

Justification / Factors influencing whether a feature was in or out of scope: ...TBD...

In Scope

Features that are planned to be implemented and fully[ish] functional.
  • Sort: Ability to sort (ascending and descending) company list by:
    • Name, alphabetical
    • Location - room/floor, alphabetical; table/booth, numeric
    • Company size/number of employees, numeric
  • Filter: Ability to filter company list by:
    • [Live] Search - start with company names only (--> might also provide sort/filter by room and floor abilities)
    • Targeted majors
    • Targeted job type/role (Intern, Coop, Full-Time, Masters)
    • Type of company / Industry categorization
    • Locations hiring - group by state or region, TBA
  • Saved user settings/preferences, so that the same search critera do not have to be repeatedly entered.
  • Ability locate a company's booth location on a map. That is, looking at a company's profile should allow you to jump to a map with a marker. This would ideally also work in reverse, selecting a table on the map could bring up the option of seeing that company profile.
  • Ability to star favorite companies
  • Ability to hide companies that are not applicable to the user from cluttering up / displaying on the map

Out of Scope

Due to the limitations of this being an independent study, as well as time constraints, the following features will intentionally be not considered in order to focus on delivering a more manageable and higher-quality set of in-scope features.
  • Designing the user experience / user interface for non-students, i.e.:
    • Recruiters / Company representatives
    • SWE student coordinators / planners of IDay
    • Potential high-level stakeholders, e.g. McCormick, University Career Services, etc.
While out of scope for functionality implementation, it is possible that I will be able to prototype the experience of some of the below features. As the project evolves, this may change.
  • Ability to easily import new data into the application for non-techy people (i.e. event planners) - this allows the app to be reused with little additional effort
  • Minimize internet usage
